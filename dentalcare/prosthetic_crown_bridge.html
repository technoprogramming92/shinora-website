What Is Prosthetic Crown and Bridge?

Treatment Details

How it's done?
One kind of dental prosthetic used to cap or cover a broken tooth is a dental crown. They can also be utilized to restore the natural appearance of your smile and fix a broken or malformed tooth. Dental bridges are comparable in that they restore the bite and fill up any gaps left by lost teeth.
Benefits:
Protection, Restoration, Functionality

Description

Dental crowns, sometimes referred to as dental caps, are artificial teeth that are custom-made to fit over an implant or existing tooth.

They offer a robust and long-lasting coating that conceals any flaws and shields the tooth from additional harm.

Dental bridges are a practical method of tooth replacement. They are composed of two crowns, one on each side, and an artificial tooth, typically constructed of porcelain or ceramic.

The crowns then hold the artificial tooth in place, giving it support and filling up the space left by the lost tooth.
