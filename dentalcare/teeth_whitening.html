What Is Teeth Whitening?

Treatment Details

How it's done?
These treatment remove and disintegrate stains from the teeth using hydrogen or carbamide peroxide. Surface stains, extrinsic stains, respond well to professional whitening. However, certain whitening solutions can also get rid of deep stains on teeth, or inherent stains.
Benefits:
Brighter smile, enhances appearance, stain removal, speedy results, long-lasting effects.

Description

Teeth whitening treatment uses a gentle yet effective process to lighten the color of your teeth and remove stains. First, we prepare your teeth by ensuring they are clean and free of debris.

Then, we carefully apply a whitening gel containing hydrogen peroxide or carbamide peroxide. This gel penetrates the enamel, breaking up stains and discoloration.

To accelerate the process, a special light or laser is used, which activates the gel and enhances its stain-fighting capabilities.The entire procedure takes about an hour, and you can relax comfortably while the gel does its work.

How to Prepare for Teeth Whitening?

Before the whitening treatment, a routine dental check-up and cleaning is done . This ensures that the teeth are free of plaque and tartar, providing a clean surface for the whitening gel to work on.

If you have any cavities, gum disease, or other dental problems, have them treated beforehand. Whitening treatment is best when the teeth and gums are healthy.

On the day of your appointment, brush and floss your teeth thoroughly to ensure there is no food debris or plaque buildup.

What to Expect During Teeth Whitening treatment?

After the initial examination, the dentist will prepare you for the whitening process by placing a protective barrier or rubber shield to cover your gums and lips. This barrier is designed to prevent the whitening gel from coming into contact with sensitive tissues.

The dentist will then apply a whitening gel, usually containing hydrogen peroxide or carbamide peroxide, onto your teeth. Depending on the whitening system used, a special light or laser may be used to activate the gel and accelerate the whitening process.

Generally, depending on the particular treatment plan, the full operation takes 30 to 60 minutes.
