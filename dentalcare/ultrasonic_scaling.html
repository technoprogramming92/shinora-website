What Is Ultra Sonic Scaling?

Treatment Details

How it's done?
Ultra sonic scaling make use of soundwaves to eliminate plaque and tartar from teeth surfaces, effectively preventing gum disease.
Benefits:
Efficient, Thorough, Comfortable

Description

By ultrasonic scaling, scaler can access areas that hand tools cannot reach. The Cavitron can reach spaces between the teeth and gums of 4mm or more.

It uses specially designed tips to get into corners and crevices, ensuring a thorough cleaning of often-missed areas.

A key feature of Cavitron systems is their non-sharp edges, which reduce or eliminate the scraping sound typical of traditional hand dental instruments, providing a more comfortable and pleasant experience for most patients during scaling.

How to Prepare for Ultrasonic Scaling?

Preparing for ultrasonic scaling is straightforward and requires minimal effort. Start by maintaining good oral hygiene practices, such as brushing and flossing your teeth thoroughly before your appointment.

This helps create a cleaner environment for the procedure. Inform your dentist about any medical conditions or medications you are taking, as this information can impact your treatment.

It's also a good idea to avoid eating large meals right before your appointment to ensure comfort during the procedure. If you have any concerns or questions about the process, discuss them with your dentist beforehand to ensure you feel prepared and at ease.

What to Expect During Ultrasonic Scalling?

During this procedure, your dental provider begins with an initial examination to assess your mouth, teeth, and gums for plaque and tartar buildup.

Protective eyewear is often provided, and a local anesthetic may be applied for comfort.

The process starts with the use of an ultrasonic scaler, a hand-held device with a vibrating metal tip that breaks up and dislodges tartar and plaque.

A continuous water spray cools the tip and flushes away debris. After the bulk of the tartar is removed, manual dental instruments such as scalers and curettes are used to eliminate any remaining small pieces of tartar and smooth the tooth surfaces. The gum pockets and mouth are then thoroughly rinsed with water or an antiseptic solution to remove any lingering debris and bacteria.

Your teeth may be polished to smooth the surfaces and deter future plaque accumulation. A post-treatment examination ensures that all tartar and plaque have been effectively removed, and your provider will offer instructions for maintaining oral hygiene and discuss any necessary follow-up care.

After the procedure, you may be advised to avoid eating or drinking for a short period and to use a special mouth rinse or toothpaste to help maintain oral health.
