What Is Composite Filling?

Treatment Details

How it's done?
Composite filling is used to stop the likelihood of a dangerous infection and to lessen the discomfort that cavities create.
Benefits:
Durability, Aesthetics, Versatility

Description

When your dentist fills a cavity, they take out any existing decaying tooth material and return the tooth to its original form.

This improves the comfort and health of your mouth.

Tooth deterioration happens gradually. Because of minute levels of mineral loss in the tooth's enamel, it first appears as white patches. Fluoride treatments or good dental hygiene can help prevent tooth decay.

However, if the enamel on your tooth keeps deteriorating, you can have a cavity that has to be filled.

How to Prepare for Composite Filling?

Before getting a composite filling, it's important to share any medical history or concerns with your dentist.

Follow any pre-care instructions provided, like avoiding eating or drinking before your appointment, Maintain good oral hygiene by brushing and flossing regularly.

What to Expect During Composite Filling?

During this procedure, composite fillings are meticulously applied in layers that gradually build up to form a durable restoration within the tooth structure.

This meticulous layering technique ensures proper adhesion and a seamless integration with the natural tooth.

As the layers of composite resin are applied, they are cured using an ultraviolet light. This curing process is crucial for bonding the filling to the tooth and ensuring its longevity. The ultraviolet light activates the chemicals within the composite resin, causing it to harden and form a strong, durable bond with the tooth structure.

Composite fillings are composed of a mixture of acrylic resin and finely polished glass particles, giving them a natural appearance that closely resembles tooth enamel.

This tooth-colored material allows for a cosmetically pleasing restoration that seamlessly blends with the surrounding teeth, making it virtually indistinguishable from natural tooth structure.
