What Is Tooth Jewellery?

Treatment Details

How it's done?
Tooth jewelry is a decorative element that is affixed to your teeth. It usually consists of tiny crystals or jewels that are attached by little glue on the tooth.
Benefits:
Expressive style, unique look, easy removal, customizable design, aesthetic enhancement.

Description

For optimal stickiness and long-lasting effects, a tooth surface must be strong and clean. It is recommended to brush and floss well the day of the appointment in order to get rid of any last bits of plaque or food debris.

Ensuring the jewelry remains in position, this procedure strengthens the adhesive's attachment to the tooth.

Making the proper design choice is also quite important; there are a lot of different designs, forms, and colors to choose from, so it's worth thinking about which one best suits your tastes and sense of style.

For maintenance of the jewelry and dental health following application, a dentist can provide advise on appropriate alternatives and aftercare tips.

How to Prepare for Tooth Jewellery?

Before the treatment, a routine check-up will be done to ensure that teeth and gums are healthy. This helps identify and resolve any issues like cavities or gum disease before applying tooth jewelry.

Decide the type of tooth jewelry. Consider styles, shapes, and colors that suit your personality.

Maintain good oral hygiene leading up to appointment. Brush and floss regularly to keep the teeth clean and ensure a smooth surface for the adhesive.

On the day of the procedure, avoid eating or drinking anything that could stain the teeth. This ensures a clean surface for the jewelry and helps the adhesive bond properly.

What to Expect During Tooth Jewellery?

The dentist starts by cleaning the tooth to remove any plaque or debris, ensuring a smooth surface for the jewelry.A mild acid solution might be applied to slightly etch the tooth, enhancing the adhesive's grip.

A small amount of dental adhesive is applied to the chosen spot on the tooth.The tooth jewelry, typically a small gem or crystal, is carefully positioned on the adhesive.The dentist ensures the jewelry is aligned and straight before proceeding.

A special curing light is used to harden the adhesive, securing the jewelry in place. The dentist makes any final adjustments to ensure the jewelry is secure and comfortable.
