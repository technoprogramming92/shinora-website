What Is Surgical Scar Reduction?

Treatment Details

How it's done?
Scar reduction surgery is a medical or cosmetic procedure used to lessen scar discomfort and appearance. A medical professional removes the scar from your skin during surgery, and they then suture the incision up.
Benefits:
Improved appearance, Smoother skin, Confidence boost.

Description

Scars are marks left on your skin after wounds heal. Scar reduction is a surgery to lessen their appearance.

There are numerous ways to reduce scars: from topical treatments to surgical procedures.

Reduction of the scar is intended to bring it closer to the surrounding skin's color and texture.

How to Prepare for Surgical Scar Reduction?

Consume a diet high in vitamins, minerals, and zinc, since these nutrients aid in the healing of wounds.

Give up smoking as it might impede the healing of wounds. Give up using anticoagulants, which are drugs that stop blood clots.

Before having their scar reduction surgery, those with diabetes might need to adhere to certain guidelines.

In addition, you might have to postpone therapy while undergoing scar reduction surgery if you're on accutane or other drugs for severe acne.

What to Expect During Surgical Scar Reduction?

During surgery, a medical professional will remove the scar from your skin, and the incision will be sutured up.

W-plasty and Z-plasty are two common scar repair procedures. A "Z" or "W" incision made by the surgeon allows the damaged skin to recover with more mobility in that location.

If there isn't enough healthy skin to seal the wound, some patients require skin transplants. Your skin may be used by a surgeon from another part of your body. A frequent therapy for severe burn scars is grafting.

In order to speed up the healing of a recent surgical incision and lower the chance of scarring again, your healthcare professional may suggest injections or topical scar therapy.
