Description

Warts

On your skin and mucosa, such as within your mouth, warts are benign growths that can appear. Warts are brought on by the human papillomavirus, or HPV. Only a small number of HPV subtypes (strains) can result in warts on certain body regions, out of the virus's abundance.

Although they can occasionally cause pain, warts are mostly harmless.

Moles

Moles can range in color from your original skin tone to pink, brown, or black, and they often first emerge in childhood or adolescence.

Compared to persons with lighter hair or complexion, those with darker skin tones typically have darker moles. Moles may be elevated or flat on the skin's surface.

Your dermatologist will either shave or chop a mole from your skin during a mole removal treatment.
