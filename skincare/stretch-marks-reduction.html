What Is Stretch Marks Reduction?

Treatment Details

How it's done?
Microneedling, retinol creams, dermabrasion, and laser skin resurfacing are among the available treatment methods for reduction of stretch marks.
Benefits:
Smoothing, Renewal, Rejuvenation

Description

Stretch marks are lines that seem scar-like, pigmented, and depressed in your skin. They appear when your skin suddenly contracts or swells.

You may notice a small ridge or indentation on your skin when you touch them with your fingers. They might also experience soreness or itching.

Your health is not compromised by stretch marks. Similar to other scars, they might not go away, but they might also change and diminish with time. there are several treatment available for reduction of stretch marks.

How to Prepare for Stretch Marks Reduction?

Stretch marks are usually easy to diagnose, and medical assistance isn't always needed. Often, identification can be made just by visual inspection.

If you do, however, decide to consult a doctor, your physician will verify the diagnosis by doing a physical examination and going over your medical history.

In certain situations, additional testing could be recommended if underlying illnesses such Cushing's syndrome are suspected. These examinations aid in the exclusion of any possible medical conditions and guarantee that the stretch marks are treated properly.

What to Expect During Stretch Marks Reduction treatment?

Stretch marks can be difficult to treat, and although they can look better after therapy, they might not go away entirely. Early treatment of stretch marks produces the best results because deeper and older blemishes can be more difficult to treat. Dermabrasion, microneedling, laser skin resurfacing, and retinol cream use are among the available treatment methods.

Laser Skin Resurfacing: This surgical technique uses concentrated light beams to precisely remove skin layers, promoting the growth of new collagen to create smoother skin.

Dermabrasion: This procedure removes layers of skin to enhance texture and shape by using a specialized device. Complete recovery occurs over a few weeks or months, and healing takes roughly two weeks.

Microneedling: For tighter skin, tiny punctures made by tiny needles stimulate the creation of collagen and elastin. Full improvements usually appear after four to six months of treatment, usually requiring many sessions. Swelling, discolouration, and itching are possible side effects.

Retinol Creams: With consistent usage, these over-the-counter, vitamin A-containing creams improve the texture and appearance of wrinkles on the skin. It could take six months to see benefits.
